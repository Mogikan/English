# English Tenses: A Complete Guide

English has 12 primary tenses, divided into four main time frames: **Present**, **Past**, **Future**, and their **Perfect** and **Continuous** aspects. Understanding these tenses is crucial for expressing time, duration, and the relationship between actions.

## 1. Present Tenses

### 1.1 Present Simple
Used for habits, general truths, fixed schedules, and permanent situations.
*   **Form:** Subject + base verb (add -s/-es for he/she/it)
*   **Example:** She **works** in a bank. / The sun **rises** in the east.

### 1.2 Present Continuous (Present Progressive)
Used for actions happening now, temporary situations, and future arrangements.
*   **Form:** Subject + am/is/are + verb-ing
*   **Example:** I **am reading** a book right now. / They **are visiting** us next week.

### 1.3 Present Perfect
Used for actions that happened at an unspecified time in the past, experiences, or actions that started in the past and continue to the present.
*   **Form:** Subject + have/has + past participle
*   **Example:** I **have seen** that movie. / He **has lived** here for ten years.

### 1.4 Present Perfect Continuous
Emphasizes the duration of an action that started in the past and continues to the present (or just stopped).
*   **Form:** Subject + have/has been + verb-ing
*   **Example:** She **has been studying** all morning. / It **has been raining**.

## 2. Past Tenses

### 2.1 Past Simple
Used for completed actions at a specific time in the past.
*   **Form:** Subject + verb in past simple (regular: -ed / irregular forms)
*   **Example:** I **finished** my homework yesterday. / We **went** to Paris last summer.

### 2.2 Past Continuous (Past Progressive)
Used for actions that were in progress at a specific moment in the past, or to set the scene for another action.
*   **Form:** Subject + was/were + verb-ing
*   **Example:** I **was sleeping** when the phone rang. / At 8 PM, they **were having** dinner.

### 2.3 Past Perfect
Used for an action that was completed before another action or time in the past.
*   **Form:** Subject + had + past participle
*   **Example:** She **had already left** when I arrived. / After they **had eaten**, they went for a walk.

### 2.4 Past Perfect Continuous
Emphasizes the duration of an action that was completed before another past action.
*   **Form:** Subject + had been + verb-ing
*   **Example:** He **had been waiting** for an hour before the bus finally came.

## 3. Future Tenses

### 3.1 Future Simple (with "will")
Used for spontaneous decisions, predictions, promises, and future facts.
*   **Form:** Subject + will + base verb
*   **Example:** I think it **will rain** tomorrow. / I **will help** you with that.

### 3.2 Future with "going to"
Used for plans, intentions, and predictions based on present evidence.
*   **Form:** Subject + am/is/are going to + base verb
*   **Example:** Look at those clouds! It **is going to rain**. / I **am going to visit** my grandparents.

### 3.3 Future Continuous (Future Progressive)
Used for actions that will be in progress at a specific time in the future.
*   **Form:** Subject + will be + verb-ing
*   **Example:** This time tomorrow, I **will be flying** to Madrid.

### 3.4 Future Perfect
Used for actions that will be completed before a specific time or another action in the future.
*   **Form:** Subject + will have + past participle
*   **Example:** By next year, I **will have finished** my degree.

### 3.5 Future Perfect Continuous
Emphasizes the duration of an action up to a specific point in the future.
*   **Form:** Subject + will have been + verb-ing
*   **Example:** In December, we **will have been living** here for five years.
